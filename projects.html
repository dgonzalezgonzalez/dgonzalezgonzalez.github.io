OECD consultancy

Demand-side policies for sustainable food choices

Consulting work for the OECD Trade and Agriculture Directorate on sustainable food choices, policy design, environmental impacts, and consumer behavior. This builds on earlier OECD work on food loss and waste, where I worked with Aglink-Cosimo scenarios, policy feasibility, and consumer behavior evidence for publications aimed at policy audiences.

I also developed a Python RAG tool using local LLM and embedding models to structure policy information and construct semantic policy-alignment indices for further analysis. The work combines policy synthesis, data processing, AI-assisted evidence extraction, and quantitative analysis for agriculture and food policy questions.

Fun GitHub projects

  • faolex-analysis - exploratory data work on legal and policy information from FAOLEX, focused on turning text-heavy institutional material into structured evidence that can be queried and analysed.
  • canarias-uni-ml - machine-learning pipeline matching web-scraped job postings in the Canary Islands to Spanish university degree programs using text embeddings and semantic similarity scoring.
  • prestaciones-por-empleo - unemployment-benefits data work with detailed eligibility-cause, demographic, and territorial breakdowns, designed to support descriptive analysis and dashboard-style exploration.
  • replication-tracker-jpe-ej-ectj - tracker for replication and reproducibility workflows across journal data checks, reflecting my work validating code, data, and empirical results in Stata, R, Python, Matlab, Julia, and related tools.
  • codex-claude-setup - personal setup experiments for agentic coding workflows, including practical configuration notes for using coding agents in empirical and policy-analysis projects.
