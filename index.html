Diego González González

PhD Candidate in Economics, Universidad Loyola Andalucía

I am an economist based in Spain. My work sits at the intersection of applied microeconomics, public policy evaluation, agri-food and food systems, and reproducible empirical research. I am especially interested in using careful data work, econometric methods, and transparent computational workflows to produce evidence for policy audiences.

I am a Junior Analyst at AIReF, a Data Replicator for the Journal of Political Economy, and an Applied Economist at Pareto.AI. I have also worked extensively with the OECD Trade and Agriculture Directorate, first as an intern and later as a consultant, contributing to analysis on food loss and waste, sustainable food choices, Aglink-Cosimo scenarios, dashboards, and AI-assisted policy evidence tools.
